PyNoetic: Modular Python Framework for Rapid Prototyping and Development of Brain Computer Interfaces

Gursimran Singh1, Aviral Chharia2, Rahul Upadhyay1, Vinay Kumar1, Luca Longo3
1Thapar Institute of Engineering & Technology, 2Carnegie Mellon University, 3TU Dublin

Abstract

Neurotechnologies, notably Brain Computer Interfaces (BCIs), have gained significant attention in recent years, due to their potential to revolutionize several domains, ranging from gaming, entertainment and robotics to medicine and rehabilitation. Nevertheless, a fundamental challenge in the rapid development of BCI systems is the absence of comprehensive software solutions specifically tailored for advancing cognitive neuroscience experiments. Existing BCI framework suffer from critical limitations, including a lack of "stage-wise" flexibility crucial for experimental research, elevated costs stemming from reliance on proprietary software, and an absence of all-inclusive features leading to reliance on multiple softwares. This work introduces PyNoetic, an end-to-end python framework that not only addresses these inherent limitations but also offers an extensive selection of methods, coupled with user-friendly compatibility for integrating novel algorithms at each developmental phase of a BCI system. PyNoetic offers a comprehensive suite of functions ranging from: stimuli presentation, data acquisition, channel selection techniques, filters, multiple feature extraction techniques, artifact removal, a wide array of analytical tools, brain-connectivity measures, machine learning (ML) classification models, data visualization, systematic testing via simulation, and evaluation of novel paradigms. To provide flexibility for research, the option to add custom functionality through scripting is provided for experienced users. PyNoetic exhibits versatility and suitability for both offline and real-time deployment of BCI systems. Adhering to stringent software development protocols, PyNoetic has been optimized with a pronounced emphasis on flexibility, modularity and scalability. PyNoetic caters to both experienced programmers and those new to Python, expediting the experiment design process. This empowers researchers to concentrate on the intricate facets of BCI development, thus accelerating future research endeavors. PyNoetic is made freely available open-source under GNU General Public License for non-commercial use.
